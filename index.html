AI Model Accuracy Liability & Food Waste Prediction Risk

FoodFlow AI makes explicit claims of 80%+ waste prediction accuracy and 15-25% waste reduction guarantees in customer communications and pitch materials. If the ML model underperforms in production (accuracy <75%), customers will see promised ROI fail to materialize, triggering refund demands and breach-of-contract claims. Legal agent generated Terms of Service but explicitly did NOT include liability caps, accuracy disclaimers, or professional indemnity insurance requirements. The platform positions waste reduction as definitive business outcome, not advisory guidance—creating unlimited liability exposure if predictions fail at scale.

Mitigation: (1) Immediately engage professional liability insurance broker for $5M+ E&O coverage before customer onboarding. (2) Legal must complete liability waiver explicitly stating FoodFlow recommendations are predictive guidance, not guarantees—customer retains final ordering/waste management decisions. (3) Implement contractual accuracy disclaimer: 'Model accuracy varies by restaurant type (QSR vs. fine dining); 80% accuracy is target, not guaranteed.' (4) Establish independent quarterly ML model validation against holdout restaurant dataset with published accuracy benchmarks. (5) Cap contractual liability at 12 months of fees paid ($14.4K max exposure per customer). (6) Create versioned model audit trail with documented prediction confidence scores for each recommendation delivered to customer.

Data Privacy & Restaurant Operational Data Security Gap

FoodFlow AI integrates with POS systems, supplier networks, and requires real-time inventory data from restaurants—inherently capturing sales patterns, menu mix, supplier pricing, and operational metrics that restaurants consider confidential competitive information. Legal agent generated Privacy Policy and DPA documents but engineer agent status is 'error'—no technical roadmap exists for data encryption, access controls, or secure data handling. Platform must comply with GDPR (if international expansion) and restaurant data residency expectations. No engineer dashboard output confirms encryption standards, data retention policies, or secure deletion procedures. Restaurants will not adopt if data privacy/security posture is unclear or non-compliant.

Mitigation: (1) Engineer agent must immediately deploy encrypted data pipeline: TLS 1.3 in-transit encryption, AES-256 at-rest encryption for all customer operational data. (2) Implement role-based access control (RBAC) with audit logging for all data access; restaurant operators control who sees what. (3) Engineer dashboard must expose data governance controls: customer-initiated data deletion, automatic 90-day data purge, data export capabilities. (4) Establish data residency compliance for EU/APAC expansion (EU data stored in EU, etc.). (5) Hire security engineer (contract basis acceptable for pre-seed) to conduct threat modeling and penetration test before beta launch. (6) Obtain SOC 2 Type II certification (not just Type I) before financial services or regulated restaurant customer wins—include data privacy audit. (7) Publish transparent Data Security Whitepaper for sales enablement; address restaurant data isolation and operational secrecy in customer contracts.
